# Last Man Running (LMR)
## Rules Authority v1.7.3

**Status:** Canonical / Locked  
**Supersedes:** v1.7.2

---

## 1. Glossary

**Arm**  
A segment of the board associated with a player, containing that player’s Point, One Spot, Home, and Home Entry.

**Base**  
The area where a player’s Pegs begin the game and where Pegs return when Killed.

**Center Spot**  
The single Spot at the center of the board. It may hold at most one Peg.

**Die / Dice**  
A six-sided die (values 1–6). Dice are always resolved individually.

**Exact Count**  
The full value of a single Die must be used to move exactly one Peg.

**Extra Dice**  
Additional Dice earned during a Turn due to specific triggers. Extra Dice follow the same rules, restrictions, and resolution mechanics as all other Dice.

**Finished Peg**  
A Peg that has reached the highest available Home Spot and may not be moved again.

**Home**  
The set of Home Spots (H0–H3) belonging to a player.

**Home Spot**  
One of the four finishing Spots for a player’s Pegs. Home Spots fill from H3 to H0.

**Kill**  
When a Peg lands on a Spot occupied by another player’s Peg, sending the other Peg back to Base.

**Kill Rolls (Optional Rule)**  
An optional rule where Killing an opponent’s Peg grants an Extra Die.

**Legal Move**  
A move that fully satisfies all applicable rules for the Die being resolved.

**One Spot**  
The Track Spot used on a roll of 1 to enter a Peg from Base.

**Peg**  
A single playing piece controlled by a player.

**Point**  
The Track Spot used to enter a Peg from Base on a roll of 6, and the Track Spot through which Center transitions occur.

**Spot**  
Any single position on the board that can hold at most one Peg.

**Track**  
The continuous clockwise path of Spots connecting all Arms, Corners, and Points.

**Turn**  
All Dice resolutions, moves, and Extra Dice taken by a player until control passes.

## 2. Setup

### 2.1 Pre-Game Configuration

Before the game starts, the following are selected and then locked for the duration of the game:

- Player colors
- Optional rules:
  - Team Play (on/off)
  - Double Dice (on/off)
  - Kill Rolls (on/off)
  - Fast Track (on/off)

### 2.2 Board Selection

The board must support the number of players:

- 2 players → 4-player board  
- 3 players → 6-player board  
- 4 players → 4- or 8-player board  
- 5 players → 6- or 8-player board  
- 6 players → 6-player board  
- 7–8 players → 8-player board  

Unused Arms remain empty.

### 2.3 Initial Peg Placement

- All Pegs begin in their player’s Base.
- If Fast Track is enabled, one Peg per player is placed in that player’s H3.

---

## 3. Core Gameplay Rules

### 3.1 Movement Direction

- Pegs move clockwise on the Track.
- A Peg may not land on or pass over a Spot occupied by one of its own Pegs.

### 3.2 One Spot, One Peg

At most one Peg may occupy any Spot at any time.

### 3.3 Exact Count

The full value of one Die must be used to move exactly one Peg.

### 3.4 Mandatory Movement

If a Legal Move exists for a Die, the player must make a move with that Die.

---

## 4. Dice Resolution

### 4.1 General Dice Rules

- Dice are resolved one Die at a time.
- When multiple Dice are available, the player chooses the order in which they are resolved.
- All Dice from a roll must be resolved or forfeited before any Extra Dice are rolled.
- No Dice may be rolled while unresolved Dice exist.
- Dice are never combined, summed, chained, or altered.

### 4.2 Extra Dice

- Rolling a **1** grants **one Extra Die**.
- Rolling a **6** grants **one Extra Die**.
- Extra Dice are granted even if the triggering Die has no Legal Move.
- Extra Dice are banked and rolled only after all currently pending Dice are resolved or forfeited.
- Extra Dice follow the same rules, restrictions, and resolution mechanics as all other Dice.
- Extra Dice may themselves earn additional Extra Dice.

If **Kill Rolls** are enabled:
- Killing an opponent’s Peg grants **one Extra Die**.
- Killing a teammate never grants Extra Dice.

### 4.3 Double Dice Mode

When Double Dice is enabled:

- The initial roll of a Turn rolls **two Dice**.
- Extra Dice earned are always **single Dice**.
- Double Dice affects only the initial roll, not the number of Extra Dice earned.
- After the initial roll, all Dice are resolved one Die at a time.

---

## 5. Legal Moves

### 5.1 Determining Legal Moves

A Legal Move must:

- Use the full value of the Die
- Obey clockwise Track movement
- Respect one Spot, one Peg
- Respect Track Entry, Center, and Home rules
- Respect all enabled optional rules

### 5.2 No Legal Move

If no Legal Move exists for a Die, that Die is forfeited.

### 5.3 Forced Moves

If only one Legal Move exists for a Die at the time it is resolved, that move is forced.

---

## 6. Track Entry

From Base:

- A roll of **1** allows entry to the One Spot.
- A roll of **6** allows entry to the Point.

Normal Kill rules apply on entry.

---

## 7. Center Spot

### 7.1 Entry

A Peg on any Point may enter the Center Spot by rolling a **1**.

### 7.2 Exit

A Peg in the Center Spot may exit to any Point by rolling a **1**.

### 7.3 Additional Rules

- Only one Peg may occupy the Center Spot.
- Kill rules apply normally.

---

## 8. Track and Home Movement

### 8.1 Track Definition

The Track is a continuous clockwise path and does not branch.

### 8.2 Home Entry

For the owning player’s Pegs:

- The Track terminates after the final Track Spot.
- A Peg may enter Home as part of a single Exact Count move.
- If Home entry is not legal, the move is illegal.

### 8.3 Finishing Pegs

- Home Spots fill in order: H3, H2, H1, H0.
- A Peg is Finished when placed in the highest available Home Spot.

---

## 9. Kill Rules

### 9.1 Where Kills Are Allowed

- Track
- Center Spot

Kills are not permitted in Base or Home.

### 9.2 Resolution

The Killed Peg returns to Base.  
The moving Peg occupies the destination Spot.

### 9.3 Kill Rolls (Optional)

If enabled:
- Killing an opponent grants one Extra Die.
- Killing a teammate never grants Extra Dice.

---

## 10. Team Play (Optional)

### 10.1 Teams

Teams are defined before the game starts and remain fixed.

### 10.2 Dice Distribution After Finishing

When a player finishes all Pegs:

- All unresolved Dice for that Turn, including banked Extra Dice, are distributed to teammates with Legal Moves.
- On future Turns, the finished player continues rolling and distributing Dice to teammates.
- Dice may be forfeited if no teammate has a Legal Move.

---

## 11. End of Game

### 11.1 Individual Play

In individual play, the game ends immediately when a player finishes all Pegs.

### 11.2 Team Play

In Team Play, **the first team to finish all of its Pegs wins, and the game ends immediately.**

### 11.3 Finishing Order

Finishing order may be recorded for summary or rematch purposes but does not affect victory.

---

## 12. Subsequent Games (Optional)

In a rematch with the same players and teams, the first finisher on the winning team may be designated to go first.
